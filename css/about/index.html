A little bit about me.

My journey into tech began with the realization that, as an active duty military spouse, the idea of having a dedicated career in one single city (or state, or country for that matter) was something I'd have to leave behind. Working as a content editor for a collector car auction company in Arizona, it dawned on me that while I won't be able to take my job with me on our next move, I could be doing SO much more than just editing — and without the restrictions of being in a dedicated office setting. I enrolled in a 10-day tech bootcamp and from there, I was hooked!

I wanted to learn not only how how to build my own site to fit my own specific needs, but everything I could about getting into tech. I decided to pursue Skillcrush's Break-Into-Tech program and when I learned how to build my first website, I realized I'd found something that gave me the freedom and versatility I was looking for in my work, and something that challenged me to develop more and more new skills. Now I'm dedicated to making the full switch to front-end development, where I can use my organization skills and eye for detail to write clean, elegant code.
